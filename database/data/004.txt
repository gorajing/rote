TASK: Send a WhatsApp Message to a Contact
PLATFORM: WhatsApp (mobile or desktop)

Step 1: Open the WhatsApp application on your device.
Step 2: Ensure you are on the "Chats" tab (bottom navigation on mobile; left sidebar on desktop).
Step 3: Locate the recipient:
   - Option A — Existing chat: Scroll or use the search icon (magnifying glass) to find an existing conversation. Tap it to open.
   - Option B — New message: Tap the compose / pencil icon (top-right on iOS; bottom-right on Android; top-left on desktop). Search for the contact by name or phone number and tap their name to open a new chat.
Step 4: Tap the message input field at the bottom of the screen.
Step 5: Type your message.
Step 6: (Optional) Enhance the message:
   - Attach a file: tap the paperclip icon and choose photo, document, or location.
   - Add an emoji: tap the emoji face icon on the keyboard.
   - Record a voice note: hold the microphone icon on the right of the input bar.
Step 7: Review the message text before sending.
Step 8: Tap the green Send button (arrow icon) to deliver the message.
Step 9: Confirm delivery:
   - One grey tick: message sent from your device.
   - Two grey ticks: message delivered to the recipient's device.
   - Two blue ticks: message read by the recipient (if read receipts are enabled).

NOTES:
- If the contact is not saved, you can start a chat by entering their number directly in the search/new-chat flow.
- To schedule a message, use a third-party automation app (e.g., SKEDit on Android); WhatsApp itself has no native scheduling.
- Group messages: tap the group name instead of an individual contact in Step 3.
